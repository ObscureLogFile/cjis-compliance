## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the minimum security requirements for the creation, use, transmission, storage, protection, and disposal of Criminal Justice Information (CJI) and Criminal History Record Information (CHRI) handled by `[[AGENCY_NAME]]`.

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that `[[AGENCY_NAME]]` maintains the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of CJI, complies with all applicable FBI CJIS Security Policy requirements, and implements administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the agency environment.

This policy applies to all workforce members, contractors, volunteers, temporary personnel, service providers, and any other person or entity that accesses, handles, stores, transmits, or supports CJI on behalf of `[[AGENCY_NAME]]`.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All information systems, devices, applications, networks, and services used to create, receive, process, store, transmit, or dispose of CJI or CHRI.
- All on-site and remote locations where CJI is accessed or stored.
- All cloud, hosted, and third-party services that connect to, store, or process CJI on behalf of `[[AGENCY_NAME]]`.
- All physical media and removable storage containing CJI.
- All users and administrators with access to CJI or to systems that support CJI handling.

If a state law, contract, court order, or agency requirement is more restrictive than this policy, the more restrictive requirement shall apply.

## 3. Authorities and References

This policy is based on and shall be interpreted consistently with:

- FBI CJIS Security Policy, current approved version
- `[[STATE_CJIS_AGREEMENT_NAME]]`
- `[[MOU_NAME]]`
- Applicable state statutes and administrative rules
- Local records retention and public information laws
- `[[AGENCY_NAME]]` information security, records management, and acceptable use policies

## 4. Definitions

- **CJI**: Criminal Justice Information, including related data that must be protected under CJIS requirements.
- **CHRI**: Criminal History Record Information, where applicable to `[[AGENCY_NAME]]`.
- **Agency Coordinator**: The individual responsible for operational coordination of CJIS-related obligations.
- **CJIS Security Officer**: The individual assigned to oversee agency compliance with CJIS security requirements.
- **System Owner**: The business owner responsible for a system that handles CJI.
- **Authorized User**: A person explicitly approved to access CJI for a documented job-related purpose.
- **Service Provider**: Any third party that hosts, stores, processes, transmits, or supports CJI for `[[AGENCY_NAME]]`.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### 5.1 Agency Head

`[[AGENCY_HEAD_TITLE]] [[AGENCY_HEAD_NAME]]` is ultimately responsible for establishing the agency’s security posture, providing resources for compliance, and ensuring that this policy is enforced.

### 5.2 CJIS Security Officer

The CJIS Security Officer shall:

- Maintain and interpret this policy.
- Coordinate agency CJIS compliance efforts.
- Review and approve security exceptions when permitted.
- Coordinate incident response activities involving CJI.
- Ensure required training, monitoring, and documentation are performed.
- Serve as the primary point of contact for CJIS security matters.

### 5.3 System Owners

System owners shall:

- Ensure systems under their control implement required CJIS safeguards.
- Maintain inventories, system descriptions, and authorization records.
- Approve user access based on job necessity and least privilege.
- Ensure required logging, retention, and monitoring are in place.

### 5.4 Information Technology Staff

IT staff shall:

- Configure and maintain technical safeguards in accordance with this policy.
- Apply patches, updates, and security configurations timely.
- Support auditing, monitoring, backup, recovery, and incident response.
- Protect administrative credentials and privileged operations.

### 5.5 Supervisors

Supervisors shall:

- Validate user access requirements.
- Notify the CJIS Security Officer of transfers, changes in duties, and separations.
- Enforce training and acceptable use requirements.

### 5.6 Workforce Members

All workforce members shall:

- Use CJI only for authorized purposes.
- Protect authentication factors and devices.
- Report suspected incidents, policy violations, and lost or stolen assets immediately.

### 5.7 Service Providers

Service providers shall comply with all CJIS requirements that apply to their services and shall support `[[AGENCY_NAME]]` audit, documentation, and incident response needs.

## 6. Policy Requirements

### 6.1 Information Exchange Agreements

Before CJI is shared with another agency, vendor, or partner, `[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall establish a written agreement that identifies:

- The purpose and authority for the exchange.
- The type of information being exchanged.
- The method of transmission and storage.
- The security controls expected of each party.
- The responsibilities for incident reporting, breach notification, and disposal.
- The review cycle and points of contact.

All exchanges of CJI shall be approved by the CJIS Security Officer and retained with supporting documentation.

### 6.2 Access Control

`[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall enforce least privilege and role-based access for all systems that handle CJI.

Requirements:

- Access shall be granted only after documented approval by the appropriate supervisor or system owner.
- Accounts shall be unique to individual users and shall not be shared.
- Administrative accounts shall be separate from standard user accounts.
- Access shall be disabled promptly upon separation, suspension, or job change.
- Accounts shall be reviewed at least every `[[ACCESS_REVIEW_INTERVAL_DAYS]]` days and whenever roles change.
- Inactive accounts shall be disabled or removed in accordance with system configuration standards.
- Session lock or automatic logout shall occur after `[[SESSION_LOCK_MINUTES]]` minutes of inactivity or a more restrictive value if required by the system owner.

Remote access to CJI or to systems that process CJI shall be controlled, logged, approved, and protected by strong encryption and strong authentication.

### 6.3 Awareness and Training

All personnel with access to CJI shall complete CJIS security awareness training before access is granted and at least annually thereafter.

Training shall include:

- Proper handling of CJI and CHRI.
- Password and authentication requirements.
- Phishing, social engineering, and malware awareness.
- Incident reporting obligations.
- Mobile device and removable media handling.
- Consequences of policy violations.

Role-based training shall be provided for administrators, supervisors, incident responders, and other personnel with elevated responsibilities.

### 6.4 Auditing and Accountability

`[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall generate, review, and retain audit logs for systems that access or store CJI.

At a minimum, logs shall capture:

- Successful and unsuccessful authentication events.
- Account creation, modification, and deletion.
- Privileged commands and access.
- Access to sensitive files, records, and databases.
- Remote access sessions.
- Security configuration changes.
- Malware detections and security alerts.

Audit logs shall be protected from unauthorized access, alteration, and deletion. Logs shall be reviewed on a regular basis and after suspicious activity, incident alerts, or significant system changes.

### 6.5 Assessment, Authorization, and Monitoring

Systems that process CJI shall be authorized before operational use and shall be reassessed on a recurring basis.

Requirements:

- A security review or assessment shall be conducted before production deployment.
- Risks, findings, and remediation steps shall be documented.
- Plans of action and milestones shall be tracked to closure.
- Continuous monitoring activities shall be established for critical systems and services.
- Significant changes shall trigger review of the authorization status.

### 6.6 Configuration Management

All systems that store or process CJI shall have approved baseline configurations.

Requirements:

- System settings shall follow approved hardening standards.
- Unnecessary services, ports, protocols, and software shall be disabled or removed.
- Changes shall be documented, reviewed, approved, tested, and tracked.
- Emergency changes shall be logged and reviewed after implementation.
- Only approved software and approved firmware may be installed on CJIS systems.
- A current inventory of CJIS-relevant hardware, software, and services shall be maintained.

### 6.7 Identification and Authentication

All users shall have unique identities and appropriate authentication methods.

Requirements:

- Passwords or passphrases shall meet agency complexity, length, and reuse requirements.
- Multifactor authentication shall be used for remote access and for any access method required by CJIS or by the system owner.
- Default credentials shall be changed before system deployment.
- Service accounts shall be documented, controlled, and used only for authorized non-human functions.
- Shared credentials are prohibited unless the CJIS Security Officer approves an exception in writing and the arrangement is technically unavoidable and documented.
- Lost, stolen, compromised, or suspected-compromised credentials shall be reported immediately.

### 6.8 Incident Response

`[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall maintain an incident response capability for events involving CJI, systems supporting CJI, or personnel with access to CJI.

Incidents include, but are not limited to:

- Unauthorized access or disclosure
- Malware infection
- Ransomware or extortion activity
- Lost or stolen devices or media
- Suspicious logins or privilege escalation
- Physical loss of records or equipment
- Failed security controls or policy violations

Incidents shall be reported immediately to `[[IR_RESPONSE_PHONE]]` and `[[IR_RESPONSE_EMAIL]]` or the designated on-call contact. The agency shall preserve evidence, limit further exposure, coordinate containment, and notify required parties in accordance with law and contract.

### 6.9 Maintenance

Maintenance on systems that process CJI shall be controlled and documented.

Requirements:

- Maintenance personnel shall be authorized and, when appropriate, escorted or monitored.
- Remote maintenance sessions shall be approved, logged, and protected.
- Diagnostic output, logs, and backups used during maintenance shall be handled as sensitive information.
- Tools used for maintenance shall be controlled and kept current.

### 6.10 Media Protection

All paper, electronic, and removable media containing CJI shall be protected throughout its lifecycle.

Requirements:

- Media shall be labeled or otherwise identified in a manner that supports proper handling.
- Storage media shall be protected from unauthorized access.
- Transport of CJI media shall use approved secure methods.
- Retention and destruction shall follow records retention schedules and destruction procedures.
- Paper records shall be shredded, pulverized, incinerated, or otherwise destroyed so reconstruction is not reasonably possible.
- Electronic media shall be sanitized or destroyed before disposal or reuse.

### 6.11 Physical Protection

Facilities, work areas, and devices that support access to CJI shall be physically protected from unauthorized access and environmental hazards.

Requirements:

- Access to areas containing CJI systems shall be limited to authorized personnel.
- Visitors shall be escorted or otherwise controlled where necessary.
- Devices shall be positioned and secured to reduce the likelihood of unauthorized viewing or removal.
- Workstations shall be placed in a manner that reduces shoulder-surfing and accidental exposure.
- Portable devices shall be secured when unattended.
- Locks, alarms, cameras, and other physical safeguards shall be used where appropriate.

### 6.12 Personnel Security

Personnel who access CJI shall be screened, trained, and removed from access promptly when no longer authorized.

Requirements:

- Background checks shall be completed in accordance with `[[BACKGROUND_INVESTIGATION_OFFICE]]` procedures and applicable law.
- Employees, contractors, and volunteers shall sign confidentiality and acceptable-use acknowledgments before receiving access.
- Access shall be removed or adjusted immediately upon reassignment, separation, or disciplinary action that affects trust or need-to-know.
- Sanctions for policy violations shall be applied consistently and may include termination, criminal referral, or civil action.

### 6.13 Risk Assessment

`[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall periodically assess the risks to systems, data, and processes that handle CJI.

Risk reviews shall consider:

- Threats and vulnerabilities
- Human factors
- Device and network exposure
- Vendor and cloud dependencies
- Physical security conditions
- Changes in business operations
- Changes in law, technology, or CJIS requirements

### 6.14 System and Communications Protection

Systems that transmit or process CJI shall protect confidentiality and integrity during transmission and storage.

Requirements:

- CJI shall be encrypted in transit over untrusted networks.
- Encryption at rest shall be used where technically feasible and where required by risk or policy.
- Firewalls, segmentation, and access control points shall be used to separate CJI environments from less trusted networks.
- Network traffic shall be monitored for unauthorized or anomalous activity.
- Security-relevant communications and alerts shall be reviewed and escalated when needed.

### 6.15 System and Information Integrity

`[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall use technical and procedural controls to prevent, detect, and respond to malicious code, unauthorized changes, and data integrity problems.

Requirements:

- Endpoint protection or comparable safeguards shall be maintained on applicable systems.
- Operating systems, applications, and firmware shall be patched timely.
- Security alerts shall be monitored and acted upon.
- File integrity or configuration integrity controls shall be used where appropriate.
- Recovery procedures shall be tested for critical systems.

### 6.16 Planning

`[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall maintain plans and procedures that support continuity of operations, disaster recovery, incident management, and restoration of systems that support CJI.

Planning documents shall identify:

- Critical functions and system dependencies
- Recovery priorities
- Backup frequency and retention
- Restoration procedures
- Alternate work locations or manual workarounds
- Roles, communications, and decision authority during disruption

### 6.17 Acquisition and Development

Security requirements shall be considered when acquiring, developing, or modifying systems and services that will handle CJI.

Requirements:

- Security requirements shall be included in procurement documents and contracts where applicable.
- Vendors shall be evaluated for their ability to meet CJIS obligations.
- Application development and system changes shall include secure design and testing practices.
- Production data shall not be used in development or testing unless approved and protected.
- Cloud and hosted solutions shall be reviewed for CJIS compatibility before use.

### 6.18 Supply Chain Risk Management

`[[AGENCY_NAME]]` shall consider supply chain risks for hardware, software, cloud services, support services, and managed services that affect CJI.

Requirements:

- Approved vendors shall be tracked and reviewed.
- Critical components and services shall be obtained from trusted sources where possible.
- Known vulnerabilities, end-of-life products, and unsupported services shall be phased out according to risk.
- The agency shall maintain awareness of vendor security notices and service changes that could affect CJI.

### 6.19 Mobile Devices

Mobile devices that access, store, or transmit CJI shall be enrolled in the agency’s mobile device management or equivalent control process if available and shall follow all approved safeguards.

Requirements:

- Only approved devices may be used for CJI access unless a documented exception exists.
- Lost or stolen devices shall be reported immediately.
- Screen locks, encryption, remote wipe, and application controls shall be enabled where supported.
- CJI shall not be stored locally on mobile devices unless approved and protected.
- Mobile device use in public areas shall be limited to reduce exposure.

### 6.20 Wireless Communications and Remote Access

Wireless access, hot spots, and remote connections that can reach CJI shall be controlled and secured.

Requirements:

- Wireless networks used for CJI shall be secured with strong encryption and authentication.
- Guest networks shall be separated from CJIS-connected resources.
- Remote access shall use approved VPN or equivalent secure transport.
- Administrative remote access shall require stronger controls than standard user access.
- The use of public or untrusted Wi-Fi for CJIS access shall be prohibited unless protected by approved secure tunneling and authentication controls.

## 7. Agency Operating Standards

### 7.1 Acceptable Use

Users shall use CJI only for official agency purposes. CJI shall not be copied, stored, or transmitted in unapproved consumer applications, personal email, or personal cloud services.

### 7.2 Password and Authenticator Handling

Users shall:

- Keep passwords, passphrases, tokens, and devices private.
- Not write passwords in visible or insecure locations.
- Change compromised credentials immediately.
- Never share authentication factors with another person.

### 7.3 Email, Messaging, and File Transfer

Any email, message, or file transfer containing CJI shall use approved systems and required encryption or other safeguards. Forwarding CJI to unauthorized recipients is prohibited.

### 7.4 Backups

Backups containing CJI shall be encrypted or otherwise protected, monitored for success, and tested for restoration. Backup copies shall receive the same level of protection as primary data.

### 7.5 Printing and Output Control

Printing of CJI shall be minimized. When printing is necessary, printed output shall be retrieved immediately and protected from unauthorized viewing or disclosure.

### 7.6 Disposal and Destruction

Records, media, and devices containing CJI shall be destroyed, sanitized, or transferred only according to approved procedures and retention requirements.

## 8. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy are discouraged. When an exception is necessary, the requesting department shall submit a written request to the CJIS Security Officer that describes:

- The specific requirement being waived
- The business justification
- The duration of the exception
- Compensating controls
- The risk to the agency

Approved exceptions shall be time-limited, documented, and reviewed before renewal. No exception may violate law or mandatory CJIS obligations.

## 9. Sanctions

Violation of this policy may result in access revocation, disciplinary action, retraining, contract termination, criminal referral, civil liability, or other remedies permitted by law and agency policy.

## 10. Incident Reporting

All suspected or actual incidents involving CJI shall be reported immediately to the supervisor, CJIS Security Officer, and IT/security contact chain.

Minimum reporting content:

- What happened
- When it happened
- Who discovered it
- What systems or records are affected
- Whether any devices or media are lost or stolen
- Whether the incident is ongoing

## 11. Records Retention

Security records, training records, audit logs, approvals, exceptions, incident reports, and assessment results shall be retained according to applicable law, contract, and agency retention schedules, whichever is longer.

## 12. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually and whenever:

- The CJIS Security Policy changes
- Significant legal or operational changes occur
- The agency introduces a new system or service that handles CJI
- A major incident indicates the policy needs revision

The CJIS Security Officer is responsible for initiating the review and proposing updates for approval.
